FIFTEEN FRAGMENTS OF EXPRESSION

Artistic categories
and medium galleries.

Discover the diverse forms of creativity that assemble to create the shared narrative of our student-led exhibition.

The Art Mediums

A presentation of fifteen individual categories, each with its unique voice.

I

Doodling

An unfiltered flow of lines and shapes, where instinct guides the hand and imagination wanders freely, turning simple strokes into unexpected expressions of creativity.

II

Photography

Each picture carrying an unspoken story, where time stands still and every frame reveals something we might have otherwise missed.

III

Canvas

Each canvas becoming a world of its own, where color, depth, and emotion blend into something that speaks without words.

IV

Poetry

An intimate blend of thought and feeling, where words become a space to release, reflect, and make sense of what feels too heavy or too complex to hold inside.

V

Abstract Art

Where expression exists without boundaries, using shape, color, and movement to evoke feeling rather than define it.

VI

Sculpture

A process of shaping and reshaping, where ideas move beyond thought and turn into something that occupies space, and invites perspective.

VII

Pencil Sketch

A space of graphite and quiet focus, where every line grows from observation, building depth, texture, and a sense of lived detail.

VIII

Pen Sketch

A raw interplay of ink and control, where each stroke is deliberate, forming precise detail and honest expression, without the comfort of correction.

IX

Watercolour

A fluid dance of water and pigment, where colours blend, bleed, and flow freely, creating soft layers of emotion and light.

X

Origami

Started from a flat sheet and somehow became something you didn’t see coming. No shortcuts, just folds and patience and a quiet kind of faith in the process.

XI

Mixed Media

Too much feeling to stay in one lane. Paint meets fabric meets found objects meets everything in between. Pieces made by people who have too much to say for one medium.

XII

Texture Art

It’s not just what you see, it’s what you almost feel. Every surface here has lived, rough in some places, tender in others, layered in ways you notice slowly.

XIII

Folk Art

Arts never meant to be kept behind glass. Mandala, Warli, Madhubani carried across generations, made for doorways and festivals and the walls of homes that held whole families.

XIV

Digital Art

Made on a screen but felt like everything else. Wondering how something built in pixels can hit this hard. It’s a new language and the artists here are already fluent.

XV

Printmaking

Every print came from pressure, from ink and intention pressed into something that holds the mark long after the hand has moved on. A rhythm repeated and deliberate.
